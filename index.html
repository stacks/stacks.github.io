What is it?

This will be a workshop in Algebraic Geometry. The intended participant (you) is a graduate student, or a post-doc, or even a senior researcher. Roughly the idea is that you will work on a single topic in a small group together with a mentor for a week with the aim of producing a text that will be considered for inclusion in the Stacks project. Part of this process will be seeing how one builds new theory from the foundations. As well, there will be one or two talks per day covering advanced topics in Algebraic Geometry.

The Stacks project workshop will have some optional activities you won't see at other workshops. Adding references to and finding mistakes in the Stacks project (and fixing them) as well as activities related to LaTeX use, Git, and GitHub. Overall these will be aimed at helping you contribute efficiently to the Stacks project.

For those who are interested, we will also discuss how it is possible to contribute to the software that runs the Stacks project (see form below).

Date and location

The workshop will be at the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor, from July 31 to August 4, 2017.
